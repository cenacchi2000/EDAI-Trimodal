Tri-Modal Severity Fused Diagnosis across Depression & PTSD

Filippo Cenacchi · Deborah Richards · Longbing Cao — Macquarie University

Key Outcomes (cross-validated)

  • Severity-aware, multi-disorder prediction (PHQ-8: 5-way; PTSD: 3-way).
  • Robust fusion vs. unimodal: stronger decision-curve net benefit; graceful under missing/noisy modalities.
  • Interpretability: SHAP shows text dominates depression; audio–face strengthen PTSD.

Abstract

We present a unified tri-modal framework synchronizing sentence-level text embeddings, log-Mel audio statistics with deltas, and OpenFace-based facial descriptors to output graded severities for depression (PHQ-8; five classes) and PTSD (three classes). Standardized features are fused via a calibrated late-fusion classifier, yielding per-disorder probabilities and feature-level attributions. On DAIC-derived corpora, fusion matches the strongest unimodal baseline on accuracy / F1 while improving decision-curve utility and robustness to missing/noisy modalities. Errors concentrate between adjacent severities; extremes are identified reliably. Attributions align with clinical markers.

Setup (fixed across folds)

Text 768-D · Audio 256-D · Face 512-D → Fusion 1,536-D (Standardize→Concat). XGBoost (multi:softprob) heads per disorder; 2k trees; lr=0.05; depth=8; subsample=0.9; colsample=0.8; λ=2. Stratified 5-fold CV with inverse class weights. Metrics: ACC, weighted-F1, AUC, MCC, κ, PR/ROC, decision-curves.
